# Reimagining Work: A People and Organizations Hackathon

**AOM 2026 Professional Development Workshop**  
**31 July-4 August 2026** at the **Academy of Management Annual Meeting** in **Philadelphia, PA, USA**  
**PDW date, time, and room:** TBD in the official AOM program

_Pre-registration is required for table matching, but all AOM attendees are welcome to attend._

This hackathon-style PDW is designed to catalyze new research on how work is being reshaped by three big shifts: algorithm-human interaction in tasks, the growing importance of social and collaborative skills, and the changing geography and form of work. Four panelists open with research seeds, and participants then work in small groups to turn those seeds into research designs, data-method pairings, and concrete next steps for publishable projects.

## Format and Structure

| Activity Description | Time |
| --- | --- |
| **Welcome and Introduction to the Hackathon Format**<br>Jillian Chown, Enrico Forti, Piyush Gulati, and Elaine Pak introduce the goals, developmental outcomes, hackathon format, and expected outputs. | **10 mins** |
| **Part 1 - Research Seed Presentations (Plenary)**<br>Each panelist gives an 8-10 minute research seed: a sharp question in search of an empirical strategy, a method looking for management applications, or a dataset with underexplored potential.<br><br>`Tristan Botelho` - How do AI-assisted human capital evaluations reshape opportunity?<br>`Seth Carnahan` - How does AI reshape organizational training systems by eliminating junior work?<br>`Maria Roche` - How long must in-person collaboration last to produce durable organizational effects?<br>`Melissa Valentine` - Org charts were developed for human decision-making; how will they evolve for AI?<br><br>Organizers close the plenary by reviewing the roundtable structure and worksheets/templates. | **45 mins** |
| **Break** | **5 mins** |
| **Part 2 - Hackathon: Parallel Breakout Roundtable Sessions**<br>Participants collaboratively develop research designs in small groups. Each table focuses on one seed, is led by a panelist, and is supported by a facilitator using a structured worksheet covering problem definition, theoretical anchors, methodological options, potential datasets, and next-step planning.<br><br>Stage 1: Idea generation and theoretical framing (35 mins)<br>Stage 2: Methods, data sources, and research design sketches (35 mins)<br>Mini break (10 mins)<br>Stage 3: Refining contributions and next steps (20 mins)<br><br>Participants are matched to panelists via pre-registration when possible; walk-in participants are welcome. Table assignments are TBD based on pre-registration. | **100 mins** |
| **Cross-table Synthesis, Wrap-Up, and Reflections**<br>One representative from each roundtable gives a 3-minute summary of the research concept, challenges, and next steps. Organizers share cross-table themes and will share compiled table notes with registered participants. | **20 mins** |

## Collaborations and Ownership of Ideas

Ideas generated during the PDW belong to the participants who developed them. Whether and how those ideas evolve into projects is up to the panelists and participants, who are responsible for appropriate authorship and crediting practices.
